Manifesto: Why This Ensemble?

The Virtual Acting Ensemble Workflow reinvents digital-located theatre. We fuse structure (Agile/Scrum backbone), co-creation (Devised), social vision (Applied Theatre), creative iteration (Design Thinking), and deep character work (Chubbuck). Every tool and milestone comes from a real discipline—no generic steps. The mission: a modular, transparent, and sustainable process giving every artist creative agency, every team clarity, and every project true engagement—wherever, whenever.

Decentralized Authority, Unique Artist Producement. Every voice matters. Cybernetic, innovative, collective, and bold.

Scrum Artist Daily/Weekly Path

1) Daily: Self-tape using Quick Character Build (3-Step DT + Chubbuck).

2) Weekly: Join Storyboard Jam (5-Step DT), Acting Sprint (character deep dive), share all discoveries.

3) Biweekly: Participate in Vision Alignment Review to align personal and group goals.

4) Result: Feedback and progress are visible and cumulative.

Core Method Cards

Plot–Story Design (5-Step DT)

  • Empathize: Understand audience/context
  • Define: Story/scene goal
  • Ideate: Brainstorm new plot
  • Prototype: Try scenes
  • Test: Review, feedback, iterate

Used in: Storyboard Jam, Creative Cycle

Quick Character Build (3-Step DT)

  • Empathize: Character emotion/context
  • Define: Wants, obstacles
  • Ideate: Tactics, actions

Used in: Acting Sprint, Scrum Artist, Daily Self-Tape

How can VR Theater redefine Participation?

Core Problem: Losing physical co-presence weakens the feedback loop. Co-presence is essential (Fischer-Lichte, 2008), but "liveness" can exist in mediatized culture without physical togetherness (Auslander, 2005).

20th Century Paradigm Shift

Meyerhold, Fuchs and Artaud (Fischer-Lichte, 1997) moved the audience from passive observers to active co-creators. These twentieth-century theories map perfectly to Burdea & Coiffet's 3I Model: what seems innovative about VR theater is actually the technical realization of what these early theorists were already hoping for.

Case Study: Bloody Broadcast

Bloody Broadcast (Kanlı Yayın) served as a live lab during the pandemic, revealing the missing real-time feedback loop in a fully digital stage setting.

Practical Focus: Demonstrates how theory works in practice and highlights gaps that next-generation VR theatre teams can solve. Acts as a modular testbed for future workflows.

Apple Vision Pro: Closing the Digital Feedback Loop

When your eyes become the stage

Hawthorne's Digital Located Theater: the entire stage exists digitally. In VR theatre, physical co-presence is lost — but real-time eye tracking can rebuild it digitally. Apple Vision Pro's spatial computing and encrypted gaze data extend audience attention while pupillometry measures live focus shifts.
