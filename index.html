Case Study: Kanlƒ± Yayƒ±n Analysis

Digital Theatre Laboratory

Pandemic-era digital performance revealing new audience-performer dynamics and engagement patterns

Audience Demographics & Behavior

  • 99% viewers from Turkey
  • Main demographic: 18-24 years old
  • Mobile: quick views; Computer: longer sessions
  • Engagement peaked during active production
  • Sharp drop after March 2021 (content stopped)

Digital Theatre Insights

  • Flexible, unscripted format enabled real-time adaptation
  • Live chat blurred performer/spectator boundaries
  • Technical barriers limited sustained feedback
  • Small loyal audience continued with archived content

Critical Discovery

Digital located theatre cannot fully replicate physical stage, but enables new rituals, audience-driven meaning, and experimental feedback systems. Sustainable digital theatre requires agile, improvisational workflows and direct, real-time audience connection.

Key Findings & Discussion

Core Discovery: Fragile Digital Feedback Loop

Fischer-Lichte's feedback loop is fragile in digital space; Auslander's "liveness" rarely achieved. Real-time feedback unsustainable with current tech (Zoom/YouTube limitations). Physical co-presence absence breaks the essential audience-performer energy exchange.

Audience Engagement Patterns

  • 8,095 total views, 773 watch hours
  • 18-24 largest group; 25-34 most loyal
  • Women watched longer sessions
  • Engagement peaked during pandemic production
  • Sharp decline after content cessation

Technical Infrastructure Barriers

  • Connection issues broke performance flow
  • Synchronization problems affected motivation
  • Chat-based interaction remained superficial
  • Production discontinuity killed team motivation
  • Limited archival value without new content

Community & Participation Desire

"I want to participate" chat comments signal engagement with 3I model. Audience-performer interaction shows community-building desire, but limited by technical constraints.

Feedback Loop Restoration Potential

The real-time feedback loop lost in digital space can be partially restored through Vision Pro's attention tracking, realistic environments, and co-presence technologies (Fischer-Lichte, 2008, p.38-40).

Conclusion

Main Contribution: Digital Theater Evolution

Audience-performer relationship in digital theater is fundamentally different from traditional stage. Digital theater is evolving‚Äîaudience is now a co-creator, not just a spectator.

Key Barriers Identified

  • Unsustainable feedback loops
  • Lack of team/audience continuity
  • Technical infrastructure fragility
  • Limited embodied participation
  • Superficial interaction models

Vision Pro as Solution

  • VR-driven real-time feedback systems
  • New managerial and technical solutions
  • Creative models combining technology and human factors
  • Sustained team motivation through continuity
  • Broader, more diverse digital communities

Future Directions

Despite current limitations, active participation and collective creativity are possible even without VR hardware. Kanlƒ± Yayƒ±n shows digital participation is achievable, but requires new creative, technical, and organizational models for sustainable digital theater.

Scrum Artist Daily/Weekly Path

1) Daily: Self-tape using Quick Character Build (3-Step DT + Chubbuck).

2) Weekly: Join Storyboard Jam (5-Step DT), Acting Sprint (character deep dive), share all discoveries.

3) Biweekly: Participate in Vision Alignment Review to align personal and group goals.

4) Result: Feedback and progress are visible and cumulative.
